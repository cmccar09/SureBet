  • What winning horses had: Yorkshire Glory, Stanners Glen all had proven race records — total_wins high, going_suitability confirmed, cd_bonus triggered by actual wins

Pattern Insight: What Discriminates Winners From Losers

Across the March 23–24 data, winners shared a distinct cluster of signals:

  • Market backing — all three winning horses fired market_leader (+12 for favourite, +5 for 2nd choice). None of the March 24 losses had it. This is the single clearest discriminator in this sample.
  • Going suitability + form wins combination — winners had both going_suitability AND high total_wins. Losers often had one but not both.
  • Trainer reputation — Stanners Glen's winning run was supported by a reputable trainer. Knock Off Soxs and Mr Nugget had no trainer signal.
  • Losses relied on softer signals — age_bonus, optimal_odds, and sweet_spot contributed heavily to losing picks. These signals are informative but not sufficient alone.
Recommendation: The market_leader signal (hardcoded at 12 pts in complete_daily_analysis.py) is doing useful work. Consider whether picks without ANY market_leader signal (i.e., not favourite or close 2nd) should require a higher base score (e.g. ≥90 rather than 85) before being displayed.

Recommendations for Future Refinement

Suggestion 1 — Market-backed threshold
Picks with no market_leader signal (horse not favourite or close 2nd) should require a higher confidence score before display — e.g. raise the minimum to 90 instead of 85 for non-market-backed selections. This would have removed Knock Off Soxs (88) from the March 24 display.
Suggestion 2 — Trainer reputation as a gate
Consider requiring at least one of: market_leader > 0, trainer_reputation > 0, or cd_bonus > 0 to qualify for display. Mr Nugget had none of these contextual signals despite high raw form score.
Suggestion 3 — Monitor age_bonus contributions
Several losses scored well partly because of age_bonus (+10 pts). Age alone is not a reliable predictor — if a horse's age_bonus is a significant proportion of its total score without strong form signals, it may indicate a weaker pick.

Note: These are pattern observations from a small sample (5 picks, 2 days). Weight changes should only be locked in after validating across a larger dataset.
